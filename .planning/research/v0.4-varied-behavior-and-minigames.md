# Research: Personality-Varied Behavior and Simple Minigames for Sei

Status: research input for milestone v0.4
Date: 2026-06-25
Scope: two linked topics. (A) making LLM actions, not just speech, vary by persona and by deliberate skill level. (B) simple non-3D minigames an LLM or a per-game engine can play, with a required GeoGuessr clone.

Constraints carried throughout: Sei is a single-layer LLM loop with a closed Zod-typed action registry, multi-provider (Anthropic Claude default, plus Gemini, OpenAI-compatible, Ollama), vision-capable models supported, running in an Electron desktop app on consumer hardware. Anthropic and other hosted APIs are black-box (no activation access). Local providers (Ollama, open weights) are the only path with internal-activation control.

Style note: this is a planning doc. Plain and factual, citations inline, no rhetoric.

---

## Executive summary

1. Speech varies easily by persona. Actions and strategy converge toward a single "helpful, fair, risk-neutral" default that RLHF bakes in. This convergence is the central problem and it is documented at both the persona level (value inertia, dictator-game convergence) and the decoding level (RLHF reduces diversity).

2. Persona prompting does move actions, but only reliably when the persona is attached to an explicit objective, utility, or scoring rubric rather than adjectives. Give each character a different objective function, not a different vibe.

3. LLMs cannot reliably self-handicap to a target skill. "Play like a beginner" produces incoherent or illegal play, not believable weakness. Skill must be controlled outside the LLM, in a per-game engine knob or in sampling.

4. The strongest believability win for game opponents is human-imitation skill calibration (Maia-style) rather than a crippled superhuman engine, because human errors are position-dependent while engine-noise and epsilon-random errors are uniform and feel alien.

5. Division of labor recommendation: LLM owns speech, intent, persona flavor, and narration. A small per-game engine owns correctness and the skill dial. Reserve activation-steering personality dials for the local-model path only.

6. Ship recommendation: GeoGuessr clone (required) + Codenames co-op (Duet) + 20 Questions, with Connect Four as a fourth when a crisp weak-to-strong skill slider is wanted.

# TOPIC A: Personality-varied actions and deliberate skill variation

## A1. Persona-conditioned decision-making: does it change what the model does?

Qualified yes. Persona prompts shift choices in structured decision settings, but the effect is fragile, prompt-sensitive, and prone to reverting to a default "assistant" strategy unless tied to a payoff or objective.

### Evidence that persona changes actions, not just tone

- Phelps and Russell, "The machine psychology of cooperation: can GPT models operationalize prompts for altruism, cooperation, competitiveness and selfishness in economic games?", Journal of Physics: Complexity, 2025. Cooperative-axis personas significantly moved choices in Prisoner's Dilemma and Dictator games. Odds ratios vs a control persona: altruistic 1.53x odds of cooperating (p<0.01); competitive 0.44x (p<1e-4); selfish 0.49x (p<1e-4). Two caveats matter for Sei: effects were much stronger in one-shot games than in repeated games with feedback, and only 3 of 10 hypotheses got strong support. Newer model snapshots produced cleaner persona separation than older ones.

- Hartley et al., "How Personality Traits Shape LLM Risk-Taking Behaviour", arXiv:2503.04735, 2025. Cumulative Prospect Theory plus Big Five on GPT-4o. Openness was the most influential trait on risk propensity. Baseline GPT-4o behaves as a risk-neutral rational agent, and the personality-to-risk relationship did not generalize consistently across model versions. Risk appetite is a real, steerable axis but defaults to neutral, so you must push it.

- Serapio-Garcia et al. (Google DeepMind), "Personality Traits in Large Language Models", arXiv:2307.00184, 2023, published as a psychometric framework in Nature Machine Intelligence, 2025. In large instruction-tuned models, Big Five measurements are reliable and valid, and induced personality profiles generalized to downstream behavior (vignette tasks), not just self-report. Reliability scales with model size and instruction tuning; small models give noisy, invalid persona signals. Code: github.com/google-deepmind/personality_in_llms.

- Jiang et al., "PersonaLLM: Investigating the Ability of LLMs to Express Personality Traits", arXiv:2305.02547, 2023 (Findings of NAACL 2024). 320 Big Five personas on GPT-3.5/4. Persona-conditioned self-reports matched assigned types with large effect sizes, and human annotators could correctly infer assigned traits from a downstream story-writing task.

- Chen, Liu, Shan and Zhong, "The emergence of economic rationality of GPT", PNAS 120(51), 2023 (arXiv:2305.12763). GPT budget-allocation decisions were largely consistent with utility maximization and scored higher on rationality than humans, but exhibited lower heterogeneity than human populations. The low-heterogeneity finding is the warning: out of the box the model is a narrow, low-variance utility maximizer, so distinct personas must be engineered against this central tendency.

- Anthropic, "Persona Vectors: Monitoring and Controlling Character Traits in Language Models", arXiv:2507.21509, 2025. Persona traits correspond to causal activation directions; injecting the vector changes behavior, not just style. Confirms persona is a real, manipulable latent variable and offers an inference-time alternative to prompting (local models only).

### Failure modes: persona inert, convergence to default

- Lee, Lee and Cho, "Inertia in Moral and Value Judgments of Large Language Models", arXiv:2408.09049, 2024. Models resist changing moral and value positions under persona or value-framework prompts; value-laden choices revert to the model's foundational orientation. A character's values are the hardest axis to move with description alone.

- Einwiller et al., "Benevolent Dictators? On LLM Agent Behavior in Dictator Games", arXiv:2511.08721, 2025. Across nine models, most converged to a 50/50 split regardless of conditions, more altruistic than humans. Behavior was prompt-sensitive and unstable rather than reflecting consistent preferences. This is the RLHF "fair/nice" attractor dominating unless actively countered.

- "The Prompt Makes the Persona", arXiv:2507.16076, and the widely-reported "expert persona" backfire effect: detailed expert personas can reduce factual accuracy (prioritizing sounding correct over being correct). Keep persona flavor out of competence-critical tool logic.

### What actually makes values and goals change action choice

Consistent lesson: persona changes behavior most when attached to a decision/payoff structure, not a descriptive adjective.

1. Explicit goal/utility framing. The economic-game results show large shifts precisely because persona is evaluated against a payoff. Give each character an explicit objective ("you value hoarding rare ores above helping the player") rather than only adjectives. The model is a near-rational utility maximizer, so give it a different utility function.
2. Per-persona scoring rubric. Rubric-as-reward work ("Rubrics as Rewards", arXiv:2507.17746; "Reward Design with Language Models", arXiv:2303.00001) shows LLMs reliably act on explicit weighted criteria. Encode each persona's priorities as a small weighted rubric the model reasons over before acting (curiosity 0.5 / loyalty 0.3 / caution 0.2).
3. Constitution-style value statements (Constitutional AI lineage) make values load-bearing rather than decorative.
4. One-shot framing amplifies persona; repeated feedback loops dampen it (Phelps and Russell). In Sei's continuous loop, re-inject the persona objective into the per-turn snapshot.
5. Risk axis is real but defaults neutral (Hartley et al.). Name a target risk posture explicitly and tie it to concrete in-game decisions.
6. Bigger/instruction-tuned models hold personas more reliably. Expect Haiku and small Ollama models to show weaker, noisier persona separation than Sonnet/GPT-4-class models.

### A1 takeaways for Sei

- Do not describe personas; give them objective functions plus a 3 to 4 line weighted rubric the brain reasons over before dispatching tools.
- Expect a strong default-attractor (fair/helpful/risk-neutral). Selfish/reckless/lazy characters need the most explicit counter-framing.
- Re-assert persona objectives in the per-turn snapshot to fight drift over long interactions.
- Tie persona to tool-selection bias: a curious character weights find/goto/explore up; a cautious one weights equip/sleep/retreat up; a greedy one weights gather/container ops up.
- Differentiate the risk axis explicitly; it cheaply produces visibly different play (cave-diving vs base-building).
- Verify persona separation actually appears on Haiku/Ollama before shipping a character.

## A2. Decoding and sampling diversity: action variety vs incoherence

### Foundational methods

- Holtzman, Buys, Du, Forbes and Choi, "The Curious Case of Neural Text Degeneration", arXiv:1904.09751 (ICLR 2020). Introduced nucleus/top-p sampling. Maximization (beam/greedy) degenerates into bland repetition; the probability tail is unreliable and must be truncated. Canonical "truncate the tail, sample the head".
- Temperature: global logit scaling, the classic diversity-vs-coherence knob; past a point it produces incoherence and repetition.
- Top-k: fixed-count truncation, brittle because the right k varies with peakedness.
- Min-p sampling: Nguyen et al., "Turning Up the Heat: Min-p Sampling for Creative and Coherent LLM Outputs", arXiv:2407.01082 (ICLR 2025, oral). The key practical paper. Dynamic truncation keeps tokens with prob >= p_base * (max token prob), so the cutoff tightens when the model is confident and loosens when uncertain. Min-p preserves both quality and diversity at high temperature where top-p collapses. Validated across Mistral and Llama-3, 1B to 123B. Already shipped in Hugging Face Transformers and vLLM, so usable on Sei's Ollama/OpenAI-compat paths.
- Locally typical sampling: Meister, Pimentel, Wiher and Cotterell, arXiv:2202.00666 (TACL 2023). Samples tokens whose surprisal is near the model's conditional entropy. Principled alternative to top-p.
- Contrastive search / SimCTG: Su et al., arXiv:2202.06417 (NeurIPS 2022) and arXiv:2210.14140 (TMLR 2023). Deterministic decoding maximizing confidence minus a degeneration penalty. Benefits are model-dependent (anisotropy assumption).
- Diverse beam search: Vijayakumar et al., arXiv:1610.02424, 2016. Splits beams into diversity-augmented groups so returned candidates are genuinely different. Relevant if Sei ever samples several candidate action plans and selects among them.
- Repetition/frequency/presence penalties: Keskar et al., "CTRL", arXiv:1909.05858, 2019 (repetition penalty); OpenAI popularized the frequency (count-scaled) vs presence (binary) split. They suppress loops but can degrade quality if set too high.

### The mode-collapse problem and that it is an RLHF artifact

- Kirk et al., "Understanding the Effects of RLHF on LLM Generalisation and Diversity", arXiv:2310.06452 (ICLR 2024). RLHF generalizes better than SFT but significantly reduces output diversity. This is why every Sei character risks playing the same way; it is baked into post-training, not just decoding.
- Zhang et al., "Verbalized Sampling: How to Mitigate Mode Collapse and Unlock LLM Diversity", arXiv:2510.01171, 2025. Root cause is typicality bias in preference data. Fix is a prompt-level technique requiring no logit access: ask the model to generate k responses with their probabilities. Reported 1.6 to 2.1x diversity over direct prompting; recovers ~66.8% of base-model diversity; +25.7% human-rated diversity. Highly relevant for closed APIs (Anthropic, Gemini) where you cannot set min-p.

### Temperature and structured tool-call validity

For agents that must emit valid structured tool calls, do not rely on temperature for validity; use constrained/structured decoding (JSON mode, function-calling, grammar/schema-constrained sampling), which guarantees the sampler only emits schema-valid tokens regardless of temperature. Recent work ("The Paradox of Stochasticity", arXiv:2502.08515) reports diminished temperature sensitivity inside JSON-structured outputs. Implication for Sei's Zod registry: the grammar protects validity, so you can run more sampling entropy for behavioral variety than the naive "high temp breaks JSON" intuition suggests, provided tool arguments are schema-bounded. Decouple "be creative about which action" (high entropy) from "emit valid arguments" (schema-constrained).

### A2 takeaways for Sei

- Use min-p (~0.05 to 0.1) with moderately high temperature (~1.0 to 1.5) when you want characters to play differently, on Ollama/vLLM/OpenAI-compat providers that expose it.
- For Anthropic/Gemini (no min-p), use Verbalized Sampling at the prompt layer to recover roughly 2x behavioral diversity.
- Lean on the Zod registry as the validity guarantee so higher entropy is safe.
- Add a light repetition/frequency penalty to stop a character looping the same action; keep it low.
- Per-persona sampling profiles are a cheap differentiator: a chaotic character runs higher temperature/min-p, a methodical one lower. Action variance itself becomes a personality trait.
- Decoding knobs increase variance, not direction. Direction comes from A1 (objectives/rubrics/risk). Neither alone is sufficient.

## A3. Deliberate skill variation and believable handicapping

### Maia: human-move prediction calibrated by training data

- McIlroy-Young, Sen, Kleinberg and Anderson, "Aligning Superhuman AI with Human Behavior: Chess as a Model System", KDD 2020 (cs.toronto.edu/~ashton/pubs/maia-kdd2020.pdf). Reframes "what is the best move?" to "what move will a human at this skill level play?". Maia is an AlphaZero/Leela-style residual network (policy + value) trained by supervised learning on Lichess human games, with no tree search at inference. Skill is calibrated by which games you train on: 9 models, one per 100-Elo bin from 1100 to 1900. Each peaks at predicting moves of players at its own rating (~50 to 53% move-match) and predicts human blunders far better at the matched skill. Cleanest existence proof that believable weak-or-strong play comes from modeling humans, not crippling a strong engine.

- Maia-2: McIlroy-Young, Tang, Kleinberg, Sen and Anderson, "Maia-2: A Unified Model for Human-AI Alignment in Chess", NeurIPS 2024 (arXiv:2409.20553; github.com/CSSLab/maia2; pip maia2). One model conditioned on a target skill via skill-aware attention: skill encoded as categorical embeddings injected into attention queries. 23.3M params covering ~1100 to 2700+; average move-prediction 53.25%. Produces coherent monotonic skill scaling (probability of correct move rises smoothly with skill input on 27% of positions vs 1% for original Maia). The most directly relevant architecture for "one model, dial the opponent's strength".

### Stockfish strength-limiting knobs

From the official Stockfish wiki:
- Skill Level (0 to 20): weakens by searching only to depth = 1 + Skill Level and applying randomized bias to scores of slightly-worse candidate moves; lower level -> bigger random boost -> probabilistically skips the best move.
- UCI_LimitStrength + UCI_Elo: UCI_Elo range 1320 to 3190 (calibrated at 120s+1s anchored to CCRL 40/4), converted internally to a Skill Level. Takes precedence over Skill Level if both set.
- Per-level Elo mapping is non-linear and front-loaded: Skill 0 ~1320, 5 ~2204, 10 ~2788, 20 full strength. Lichess AI 1 to 8 maps roughly <400 / 500 / 800 / 1100 / 1500 / 1900 / 2300 / 2800+.
- Believability caveat: weakened play is random blunders injected into otherwise strong search, which can feel alien (perfect then randomly throws). The 1320 floor means Stockfish cannot natively emulate a true beginner.

### MCTS and search-budget handicaps

- For Lc0/AlphaZero-style MCTS, strength rises monotonically with node/simulation count, so reducing nodes reliably weakens play (down to 1 node = raw policy net). Temperature controls move-selection randomness.
- Ishihara et al., "Dynamic Difficulty Adjustment by MCTS for the game Pac-Man", IEEE CIG 2010 (varying max simulations). York study, "Challenging AI: Evaluating the Effect of MCTS-Driven Dynamic Difficulty Adjustment on Player Enjoyment", 2019. Demediuk et al., "Diversifying dynamic difficulty adjustment by integrating player state models into MCTS", Expert Systems with Applications 2022. Recurring finding: an agent that deliberately selects lower-evaluated moves feels more believable than one that just runs fewer simulations; starving compute yields outcome-correct but proactively-dumb play.
- de Sa et al., "Hybrid Minimax-MCTS and Difficulty Adjustment for General Game Playing", SBGames 2023 (arXiv:2310.16581).

### Classic per-game engine handicapping (reliable baseline)

- Depth-limited minimax/alpha-beta: shallower depth = weaker. Lichess uses Duration x Skill Level x depth. A blunder can be defined precisely as a move leading to a strictly worse minimax value, so you can target a blunder rate. Anderson et al., "Assessing Human Error Against a Benchmark of Perfection", KDD 2016 (arXiv:1606.04956) quantifies human blunder rates vs an oracle.
- AdaptiveMiniMax: adapts move quality to the average evaluation of the human's own moves (matches the opponent rather than a fixed Elo).
- Epsilon-greedy / blunder injection: with probability epsilon pick a random or k-th-best move; cheap and tunable but least believable, because errors are uncorrelated with position difficulty whereas humans blunder more in hard positions.

### LLMs cannot self-handicap to a target skill (do it engine-side)

- "Play like a beginner" prompting fails. Adding a beginner system prompt did not produce believably weak chess; it produced illegal/incoherent play (dynomight.net/chess; Saplin's 13-model test). LLM chess quality is brittle to phrasing, not a controllable dial.
- LLMs perceive/control difficulty weakly. "LLMs May Not Be Human-Level Players, But They Can Be Testers: Measuring Game Difficulty with LLM Agents", arXiv:2410.02829, and difficulty-control work (arXiv:2509.14545, arXiv:2502.01683) report weak correlation between target and realized difficulty.
- Sandbagging shows the failure mode directly. van der Weij et al., "AI Sandbagging: Language Models can Strategically Underperform on Evaluations", arXiv:2406.07358, 2024: models can be prompted or password-locked to hide capability, but calibrating to a precise target is hard. "LLMs Can Covertly Sandbag on Capability Evaluations Against CoT Monitoring", arXiv:2508.00943, 2025: even when trying, models succeed only 16 to 36% of the time.
- Conclusion: treat skill as an engine/sampling parameter, not something the LLM self-limits.

## A4. Hybrid behavioral diversity without heavy RL

### Quality-Diversity and MAP-Elites (repertoire generation)

- Mouret and Clune, "Illuminating search spaces by mapping elites" (MAP-Elites), arXiv:1504.04909, 2015. Discretize a behavior-descriptor space, keep the best elite per cell, output a whole map of diverse high-performing solutions. The abstraction for "a repertoire of distinct playstyles, each competent". Proven for diverse robot gaits (Cully, Clune, Tarapore and Mouret, "Robots that can adapt like animals", Nature 2015) and for procedural game content.
- Lehman and Stanley, "Abandoning Objectives: Evolution Through the Search for Novelty Alone", Evolutionary Computation 19(2), 2011 (novelty search), and the GECCO 2011 novelty + local-competition paper (conceptual root of QD).
- Relevance: an offline, no-RL way to generate a library of behaviorally-distinct strategy/prompt configs to attach to personas. Libraries: QDax (arXiv:2308.03665), pymap_elites.

### Diversity-driven RL (heavier; the "if you ever train" tier)

- Eysenbach et al., "Diversity is All You Need" (DIAYN), ICLR 2019 (arXiv:1802.06070). Learns an ensemble of distinguishable skills by maximizing mutual information between a skill latent and visited states; diverse behaviors emerge with no task reward. Template for "one policy, N switchable behavior modes".
- Parker-Holder et al., "Effective Diversity in Population-Based RL" (DvD), NeurIPS 2020 (arXiv:2002.00632). Measures population diversity via the determinant of a behavior-embedding Gram matrix, avoiding redundant clones. Useful diagnostic even without the full optimizer.

### Bandits over a discrete strategy set (lightweight adaptive lever)

The cheapest way to vary and adapt strategy per persona without training: define a finite arm set of strategies and select with a bandit.
- Thompson, Biometrika 1933 (Thompson sampling). Auer, Cesa-Bianchi and Fischer, "Finite-time Analysis of the Multiarmed Bandit Problem" (UCB1), Machine Learning 2002. Chapelle and Li, "An Empirical Evaluation of Thompson Sampling", NeurIPS 2011. Non-stationary variants (discounted/sliding-window UCB and TS; Garivier and Moulines 2011) for drifting opponents.
- Each persona gets a distinct prior over arms (an aggressive persona weights aggressive arms) while still adapting online to what works. Composes naturally with any per-game UCT/MCTS engine.

### Activation steering and persona vectors (strongest control, local models only)

These require read/write access to internal activations, so they work on local/open weights, not hosted black-box APIs.
- Turner et al., "Activation Addition / Steering Language Models With Activation Engineering" (ActAdd), arXiv:2308.10248, 2023. Steering vector = activation difference between a pair of contrast prompts at one layer, added to the forward pass. No fine-tuning.
- Rimsky et al., "Steering Llama 2 via Contrastive Activation Addition" (CAA), ACL 2024 (arXiv:2312.06681). Steering vectors averaged over many contrastive pairs, added with a tunable +/- coefficient, giving a smooth behavior knob that stacks on fine-tuning/system-prompts.
- Anthropic, "Persona Vectors", arXiv:2507.21509, 2025. Automated pipeline extracts a persona vector from a trait name + contrast prompts; supports monitoring, post-hoc steering, and preventative "vaccine" steering. A per-trait dial, but needs activation access (local models).
- "Identifying Cooperative Personalities ... through Personality Steering with Representation Engineering", arXiv:2503.12722, 2025: steering toward Agreeableness/Conscientiousness yields more self-sacrifice and lower lying rates but higher exploitability.

### Multi-agent LLM studies: personality drives behavioral divergence (API-friendly)

- arXiv:2503.12722 (above): clean cooperation/vulnerability trade-off useful for "naive vs cunning" companions.
- "Understanding LLM Agent Behaviours via Game Theory", arXiv:2512.07462, 2025, and "More at Stake: How Payoff and Language Shape LLM Agent Strategies in Cooperation Dilemmas", arXiv:2601.19082, 2026: payoff framing and language systematically shift cooperation/defection. Persona/role-differentiated multi-agent setups reach much higher strategic-behavior simulation accuracy than single undifferentiated LLMs. Persona differentiation is the cheap lever that produces behavioral diversity on plain APIs.

### A4 takeaways for Sei (tiered by deployment cost)

Tier 1, pure prompt + sampling (works on any hosted API including Anthropic):
- Persona-conditioned system prompts produce real, measurable behavioral divergence in social/strategic settings. Use distinct persona priors for cooperation/aggression/honesty.
- Temperature/top-p (or min-p on local) is the cheap diversity knob for speech and non-adversarial action choice.
- Do not rely on the LLM to self-handicap skill.

Tier 2, small per-game engine with a difficulty parameter (the reliable skill dial):
- Depth-limited minimax/alpha-beta, MCTS with capped sims, or Stockfish UCI_Elo/Skill Level for chess. Monotone, repeatable strength control.
- Prefer human-likeness over crippled-strength for believability: Maia-style models err the way humans do. If you cannot run Maia per game, at least make blunder probability scale with position difficulty and have the weak agent pick lower-evaluated moves rather than just running less search.
- Adapt to the player with AdaptiveMiniMax-style matching or a bandit over a discrete strategy set.

Tier 3, needs local/open-weights access (most powerful persona control, not on hosted Anthropic):
- Activation steering / persona vectors give a smooth composable per-trait dial. Flag as a local-model premium feature.
- Offline repertoire generation via MAP-Elites / novelty search can pre-compute a library of distinct strategy configs without RL.

One-line architecture: keep the LLM for speech, intent, and persona flavor (Tier 1); put skill in a per-game engine knob (Tier 2); reserve mechanistic personality dials (Tier 3 steering) for the local-model path. The strongest believability win is Maia-2-style human-imitation skill calibration rather than handicapped-superhuman noise.

## A5. The meta-point: when should the LLM play directly vs narrate?

Division of labor, by game type:

- Pure language/knowledge/social games (20 Questions, word association, trivia, Codenames, Pictionary describer): the LLM plays directly. Skill variation is prompt-side (information-gain scaffold on/off, memory leaks, category lanes) plus sampling. This is where personality lives.
- Exact-state / search games (chess, Connect Four, Othello, Battleship, Wordle solver): a per-game engine plays and the LLM narrates in character. Never let the LLM compute the move; feed it the engine's chosen move plus a one-line rationale and have it dramatize, not decide. LLMs lack an invariant board representation (arXiv:2512.01992).
- Vision games (GeoGuessr, Pictionary guesser): the vision LLM plays directly, but frontier models are very strong, so deliberately handicap for fun (lower resolution, thinking-time cap, casual persona) and surface the reasoning as companion chatter.

Believability rule across all: handicap by making errors position/context-dependent (human-like), not uniformly random.

# TOPIC B: Simple non-3D minigames

## B1. GeoGuessr clone (required)

Show a random street/earth scene, player and AI drop a guess on a world map, score by distance, per-round timer.

### Recommended stack

- Scene/imagery: Mapillary API v4 (free, commercial-OK, CC BY-SA, Meta-owned) as primary, with Google Street View Static API as an optional premium "HD mode" behind the developer's or user's own key.
- Random valid location: GeoNames populated-place sampling (country-weighted) + Natural Earth land rejection + imagery-existence rejection; pre-curate a vetted pano-ID pool offline.
- Guess map: MapLibre GL JS (BSD-3) or Leaflet (BSD-2) + MapTiler/Stadia key, or bundled PMTiles for offline.
- Scoring: 5000 * exp(-d_km / 2000), haversine with R=6371 km, guesses under 25 m clamp to 5000.
- AI play: feed the scene JPEG to the existing vision LLM; expect strong results, so handicap for fun.

### 1. Google Street View Static API

Big 2025 change: effective March 1, 2025, Google removed the recurring $200/month credit, replaced by per-SKU free monthly caps and a tiered model (Essentials/Pro/Enterprise). Sources: developers.google.com/maps/billing-and-pricing/march-2025 and .../faq.

Street View Static API pricing (current core-services list): Essentials tier, free cap 10,000 events/month, then per 1,000 requests $7.00 (0 to 100K) scaling to $0.53 (5M+). So ~$0.007/round beyond the free cap.

Street View Image Metadata endpoint is free and unlimited: GET maps.googleapis.com/maps/api/streetview/metadata?location=LAT,LNG&key=... returns status (OK, ZERO_RESULTS), pano_id, exact lat/lng, capture date, copyright. Canonical way to test whether a pano exists (status==OK) and snap to the real pano location before spending a paid Static request.

ToS restrictions that matter:
- No caching/storing of imagery pixels. You may NOT pre-download a library of panos to ship offline.
- Exception: pano_id may be cached indefinitely. You can store a curated list of valid pano IDs and re-fetch the live image each time. This is the supported pattern.
- No bulk scraping/pre-fetching.
- Attribution required when shown outside a Google map: display the Google/Google Maps wordmark on the image. No hard rule that Street View must sit next to a Google Map, but attribution is mandatory.
- App must publish Terms of Use + Privacy Policy.

Embed vs Static: the Embed API (iframe streetview mode) is a low/no-cost interactive Google-hosted iframe; you cannot take a single still or strip UI, awkward in an Electron renderer. Use the Static API (plain JPEG you control) gated by the free Metadata endpoint.

Verdict: best quality and global coverage, but BYO key, metered beyond 10K/mo, no-cache rule means every round is a live call plus a Google logo. Good as an opt-in HD mode.

### 2. Mapillary (API v4): recommended primary

- Owner/cost: Meta-owned since 2020; paid tier dropped, all imagery and API access free including commercial use, subject to rate limits.
- License: CC BY-SA 4.0. You may use/redistribute commercially with attribution (creator + Mapillary + link to source/license). ShareAlike applies to derivatives of the image itself; showing a pano as a round and asking for a guess does not make your whole app a derivative, but keep attribution on-screen. Materially more permissive than Google for a desktop app: you can cache, ship, and show anywhere.
- Auth: register an app, get a client access token (MLY|...), pass ?access_token= or Authorization: OAuth.
- Fetch near a coord: radius search GET graph.mapillary.com/images?lat=X&lng=Y&radius=Z (radius max 50 m); bbox search; image pixels via thumb_2048_url / thumb_original_url; many images are 360 (is_pano).
- Rate limits: Entity API 60,000/min/app; Search API 10,000/min/app; tiles 50,000/day/app.
- Coverage: crowdsourced, denser than Google in some cities, absent in many rural areas, globally far less complete than Street View; quality varies.
- Vision usability: ordinary JPEGs/360 frames, hand straight to Claude/Gemini vision.

Verdict: primary scene source. Free, commercial-OK, cacheable, no "must show a Google map" baggage. Pre-curate high-quality pano IDs offline to sidestep coverage gaps.

### 3. KartaView (formerly OpenStreetCam)

- Owner: GrabMaps (Grab Holdings). License: imagery CC BY-SA 4.0 (website code MIT). Public read endpoints need no auth.
- Coverage: ~384M+ images, ~8M km of road, heavily skewed to Southeast Asia. Mostly non-360 stills outside that region.
- Verdict: useful secondary/supplementary source, especially to boost SE-Asia coverage; not strong enough to be primary.

### 4. Random valid location with imagery (the core engineering problem)

Naive uniform lat/lng gives ~71% ocean and most land points have no pano. Layered approach:
1. Uniform-on-sphere land sampling with rejection: generate a sphere-uniform point (lat = asin(2u-1), lng uniform), point-in-polygon against Natural Earth country polygons (1:50m, ~241 countries). First-try land hit rate ~29%, so ~3.4 attempts per accepted land point.
2. Bias toward inhabited/photographed places (recommended): sample a populated place from GeoNames (cities500/cities1000, CC BY 4.0), optionally country-weighted by population or imagery density, then jitter within a few km. Guarantees nearby roads (imagery likely) and fairer rounds.
3. Reject against real imagery metadata: Google metadata endpoint (accept on status==OK, snap to pano_id) or Mapillary radius search (accept if >=1 image). Loop with backoff.
4. Pre-curation (best for shipping): run the above offline to build a vetted pool of pano/image IDs (storing IDs is allowed even under Google ToS). At runtime, pick from the pool. Deterministic, fast, hand-filterable (drop indoor/dark/blurry), country-weightable for difficulty tiers.

Datasets: Natural Earth (public domain polygons), GeoNames (CC BY 4.0 populated places), Who's On First, OSM place nodes.

### 5. Photorealistic 3D Tiles / Cesium and open options

- Map Tiles API pricing: 2D Map Tiles Essentials 100,000 free/mo then $0.60/1,000; Street View Tiles 100K free then $2.00/1,000; Photorealistic 3D Tiles Enterprise tier, only 1,000 free/mo, then $6.00/1,000. Pricey for a consumer game.
- 3D Tiles: OGC 3D Tiles (glTF), rendered via CesiumJS/deck.gl with an API key, mandatory Google attribution, same no-scrape/no-cache ToS. EEA Terms apply from 8 July 2025. Aerial 3D is a weaker GeoGuessr experience than ground-level. Treat as a novelty mode.
- Open/offline: Panoramax (open, French IGN + OSM-France, STAC API, >100M images by April 2026, CC-BY-SA photos, self-hostable; Europe-skewed). Academic datasets for fully offline play: StreetView (Zamir/Shah), Pittsburgh 250k, Mapillary Street-Level Sequences (MSLS), HF sets like stochastic/random_streetview_images_pano. Best path for offline + license-clean.

### 6. Scoring (distance to points)

GeoGuessr's published math (latb.io "The maths of GeoGuessr"):
- Distance: great-circle haversine, R = 6371 km.
- Score: S = 5000 * exp(-10 * d / D) where d = guess-to-target distance and D = the diagonal of the map's bounding box.
- Perfect: guess under 25 m (or ~1/100000 of the diagonal) -> 5000.
- Legacy world-map constant people reference: points = 5000 * exp(-d_km / 2000). GeoBench/Epoch AI uses the same family.
- Recommendation: world map -> S = 5000 * exp(-d_km / 2000), round to int, clamp under 25 m to 5000. Per-round timer just bounds time; do not fold time into score unless you want a speed mode.

### 8. How a vision model plays

- PIGEON / PIGEOTTO (Stanford, CVPR 2024; Haas, Skreta, Alberti; arXiv:2307.05845): StreetCLIP backbone + semantic geocells + ProtoNets. First CV model to reliably beat top human GeoGuessr pros. ~92% country accuracy, median error ~44 km, ~40% of guesses within 25 km. The "what's possible" ceiling, but a specialist model.
- General LLMs are now excellent. GeoBench (epoch.ai/benchmarks/geobench, leaderboard geobench.org): models output lat/lng + country, scored with the same 5000-point exponential/haversine curve. Gemini 2.5 Pro is currently the strongest LLM geolocator (reported median error ~0.8 km, ~64% within 3 km on GeoBench-style eval). Claude and GPT/o3 are competitive but generally behind. Other benchmarks: GeoArena (arXiv:2509.04334), "Where on Earth?" (arXiv:2510.10880), GeoBenchX (arXiv:2503.18129).
- Implication: Sei's existing Claude/Gemini brain can play by being handed the scene JPEG and asked for a lat/lng + reasoning, scored identically to the human. Because frontier models are very good (sub-km medians on easy maps), handicap the AI for fun (lower resolution, no EXIF, thinking-time cap, casual persona that reasons aloud and sometimes guesses wrong). The reasoning trace ("palm trees + yellow plates + right-hand drive -> likely Thailand") is great companion content and fits Sei's say()-tool scratchpad-to-chat pattern.

## B2. Other candidate games

Framing: where skill lives is the decisive lever. An LLM is strong-by-default and hard-to-handicap-believably at language/knowledge tasks and weak-and-unreliable at exact state-tracking or search. A per-game engine is the opposite: trivially tunable from perfect to terrible, no personality. Best companion games use the engine-narrator split.

### 20 Questions

- Both roles LLM-native. Answerer is trivial and reliable. As guesser, optimal play is information-theoretic. Bertolazzi et al., "Learning to Ask Informative Questions ... Expected Information Gain", arXiv:2406.17453, 2024 (DPO on EIG-ranked questions). BED-LLM, Choudhury, Williamson et al., arXiv:2508.21184, 2025 (sequential Bayesian experimental design; more than doubles success rate over naive prompting). Hu et al., arXiv:2310.01468, 2023 (20Q as a multi-turn planning benchmark).
- Skill: a clean continuum. Strong = explicit candidate set + EIG ranking or bisection prompt. Weak believably = strip the scaffold, leak/corrupt 1 to 2 prior answers from context (redundant/contradictory questions, very human), or bias toward premature specific guesses. Personality = question cadence and guess bravado.
- Build: lowest of all candidates. Turn counter + yes/no parser + two registry tools. A few hours.
- Co-op and versus, symmetric.
- Verdict: STRONG / ship-tier. Cheapest, best persona surface, believable handicap knobs.

### Word association / word chain / Shiritori

- Fully LLM-native; no engine. Validity (real word, correct starting letter, not reused) is a trivial dictionary lookup the registry owns.
- Skill is naturally bounded (no winning), so a vibe game, max personality, low competitive depth. Strong persona uses rare associations and hard-to-continue letters; weak persona uses obvious ones and occasionally fails the letter rule (engine-detectable, legible loss).
- Build: very low.
- Co-op-feeling; Shiritori gently competitive.
- Verdict: STRONG but shallow. Good ambient/secondary mode, not a flagship.

### Codenames-style

- Both LLM and classic embedding bots play. Stephenson, Sili et al., "Codenames as a Benchmark for Large Language Models", arXiv:2412.11373, Dec 2024 (GPT-4o, Gemini 1.5, Claude 3.5 Sonnet, Llama 3.1 as spymaster and guesser). LLM agents pair well together; classic word2vec/GloVe bots have limited vocabularies and fail when paired with a different-style partner.
- Two skill engines. Engine-side: embedding spymaster (cosine similarity over GloVe/word2vec/Sentence-Transformers); degrade by using a tiny/old embedding model or a high-risk threshold so it overreaches on the number. LLM-side: strong = let it reason about assassin/opponent words (theory-of-mind); weak = forbid that reasoning so it gives greedy clues that backfire. Personality rich: clue style, risk appetite, post-reveal reactions.
- Build: medium. 5x5 word grid (web-trivial), team/assassin assignment, two roles (give_clue{word,count}, guess_tile{index}), reveal logic.
- Co-op: best fit of any candidate. Official Duet/co-op mode maps perfectly onto a partner AI (Sei as your spymaster or guessing partner), directly matching the "invite/involve the player" direction.
- Embedding refs: GloVe (Pennington et al. 2014), word2vec (Mikolov et al. 2013), Sentence-Transformers (Reimers and Gurevych 2019).
- Verdict: STRONG / ship-tier. Best companion (co-op) fit, dual skill engines, rich personality, web-trivial UI.

### Wordle / Connections

- Engine plays Wordle near-perfectly (information theory / 3Blue1Brown entropy; "On Optimal Strategies for Wordle and General Guessing Games", arXiv:2305.09111; POMDP/RL treatment arXiv:2211.10298). LLMs play Wordle badly (cannot track letter-position constraints; jerpint "GPT-4 Can't Play Wordle", 2023). Connections is even harder: Samadarshi et al. "Connecting the Dots", arXiv:2406.11012, 2024 (Claude 3.5 Sonnet fully solves 18%); "NYT-Connections: A Deceptively Simple Task", arXiv:2412.01621, 2024 (top models trail humans ~30%).
- LLM weakness here is a liability, not a feature: a strong LLM mode is hard to reach and a weak mode is indistinguishable from broken. Do Wordle skill engine-side. Connections is hard to engine-ify (categories need world knowledge), so it leans LLM where even the best are mediocre.
- Build: medium for Wordle; Connections needs a curated puzzle bank (NYT licensing/originality concern).
- Both fundamentally solitaire; weak co-op shape.
- Verdict: MODERATE. Wordle only as a co-op "help me solve" assist mode. Skip Connections.

### Trivia / quiz

- LLM-native for answering and hosting. Knowledge benchmarks give the vocabulary: TriviaQA (Joshi et al. 2017, arXiv:1705.03551), MMLU (Hendrycks et al. 2020, arXiv:2009.03300, 57 subjects with difficulty levels).
- Hardest to handicap believably (a strong model knows almost everything; playing dumb reads as fake). Believable levers: restrict categories to a persona's lane (ignorance becomes characterful, not random); inject a controlled plausible-wrong-answer rate (confidently wrong like a real person); speed pressure. Player-question difficulty gated by MMLU-style level or category. Personality excellent (confidence, trash talk, pedantry).
- Build: low-medium. Question bank (license an open set like OpenTDB to avoid the model knowing its own answers), multiple-choice to sidestep free-text grading, buzzer/timer UI.
- Versus (buzzer race) is the natural strong shape; co-op also works.
- Verdict: MODERATE-STRONG. Cheap, great personality, strong versus fit; the category-lane mechanic is what makes the handicap work.

### Pictionary / drawing guessing

- Vision-guesser (player draws, Sei guesses): viable but mediocre. Best-case sketch F1 stays below ~70% ("A Vision Check-up for Language Models", arXiv:2401.01862). Quick, Draw! dataset (Google, ~50M drawings, 345 categories) and SketchRNN (Ha and Eck, arXiv:1704.03477, 2017) are canonical; a small CNN trained on Quick, Draw! is more reliable and tunable than a VLM and runs on consumer hardware. Describer direction (Sei describes, player draws) sidesteps vision and is LLM-trivial.
- Skill: classifier-side cleanly tunable; VLM hard to raise but easy to lower. Personality delightful (wrong guesses are comedy).
- Build: higher (canvas surface plus VLM round-trip or bundled CNN).
- Co-op leans well.
- Verdict: MODERATE. Highest charm and good co-op fit, but build and VLM accuracy ceiling make it risky. Describer-mode is a cheap way to capture most of the delight with none of the vision risk.

### Chess / simple board games

- Clearest engine-narrator split. LLMs play board games weakly and unreliably: "LLM Chess: Benchmarking Reasoning and Instruction-Following", arXiv:2512.01992, 2025 (o3-low ~758 Elo; only strongest reasoning models beat a random mover even when given legal moves). Contrast Othello-GPT (Li et al. arXiv:2210.13382, 2022; Nanda linear-probe follow-up arXiv:2309.00941): a purpose-trained small model builds a real world model, but a chat LLM prompted with FEN does not. Never let the LLM compute moves.
- Games with trivial/strong engines for clean skill calibration: Tic-Tac-Toe (solved), Connect Four (strongly solved; Allis 1988; Tromp), Nim (closed-form Sprague-Grundy), Checkers (Chinook, Schaeffer Science 2007), Reversi/Othello (Edax), Dots and Boxes (rich skill gradient), Battleship (probability-density targeting, human-feeling and personality-friendly), Mancala.
- Skill engine-side and perfectly tunable; personality LLM-side narration (feed move + rationale, dramatize, never decide). Battleship and Dots-and-Boxes give the most for the narrator. Connect Four and Battleship are the sweet spots. Avoid chess (intimidating for non-technical users, heavy coupling).
- Inherently versus, weak co-op fit (casts Sei as opponent).
- Verdict: STRONG for one or two specific titles (Connect Four, Battleship).

### Hangman

- Both roles LLM-viable with engine assist (optimal play is frequency/entropy over a wordlist). Skill engine-side or LLM-side. Believable weak play: uncommon letters early, panic near the limit. Modest personality surface.
- Build: very low.
- Either direction; light filler. Verdict: WEAK-MODERATE. 20 Questions dominates it on the same budget.

### Social deduction (Werewolf / Mafia / Avalon)

- Richest LLM research area. Gold standard: Meta CICERO, "Human-level play in the game of Diplomacy by combining language models with strategic reasoning", Science 2022 (doi:10.1126/science.ade9097): a 2.7B LM coupled to a strategic-reasoning engine reached top-10% on webDiplomacy. Exactly the engine-narrator split at scale. Party social-deduction: Xu et al. "Exploring LLMs for Werewolf", arXiv:2309.04658, 2023; AvalonBench arXiv:2310.14985, 2023; MultiMind arXiv:2504.18039, 2025; "Bayesian Social Deduction with Graph-Informed LMs", arXiv:2506.17788, 2025.
- Personality maximal, but skill calibration hard/noisy (no clean oracle; strong play needs the CICERO planning layer). These games need many players (5 to 10); a single companion + one human cannot make a real table without spinning up multiple LLM personas, multiplying cost/latency and conflicting with the single-companion, single-loop architecture.
- Build: highest of all candidates.
- Verdict: NOT NOW. Best personality theater, deepest research, but fundamentally multiplayer and architecturally at odds with a single-companion app. Revisit only if Sei supports a party of AI characters.

## B3. Ranked shortlist (excluding GeoGuessr, already chosen)

Ranked by easy-to-build + believable skill/personality variation + companion (co-op) fit.

1. Codenames (co-op Duet). Best companion fit, dual studied skill engines, rich personality, web-trivial grid. Skill: engine-side embedding spymaster (shrink/age the embedding model or raise the risk threshold) or LLM-side (allow/forbid assassin reasoning). Build: board generator + give_clue/guess_tile tools + reveal logic. Start with LLM spymaster for co-op Duet; add embedding-bot spymaster later as the tunable slider.

2. 20 Questions. Cheapest build, best handicap believability (information-theoretic axis + memory-leak handicaps feel human), symmetric co-op/versus, and it is a conversation. Skill: LLM-side EIG/bisection scaffold for strong, strip scaffold + leak prior answers for weak. Build: turn counter + yes/no parser + ask_question/make_guess/answer tools. Ship answerer role first (zero failure modes), then guesser with persona-selectable scaffold.

3a. Connect Four (pick for a clean demonstrable skill slider). Strongly solved, so the engine plays from perfect to random with one parameter, the most legible weak-to-strong axis in the product. Personality = LLM narration over engine-chosen moves. Skill engine-side (search depth / epsilon-greedy on the solved oracle). Build: 7x6 grid + ~150-line solver + narration tool. Caveat: versus, casts Sei as opponent.

3b. Trivia (category-lane) (pick to stay in language/co-op-versus banter). Cheap, huge personality, strong buzzer-versus. Skill LLM-side via category restriction + controlled plausible-wrong-answer rate. Difficulty gated by MMLU-style level. Risk: believable self-handicap is hardest here; use an external question set (OpenTDB) so the model does not know its own answers.

Lean recommendation: ship Codenames (Duet co-op) and 20 Questions first. Together they cost little, cover both co-op and conversational versus, and give both an engine-tuned skill slider (Codenames embedding bot) and a believably-handicapped LLM skill slider (20Q memory leak). Add Connect Four as the third when a crisp demonstrable weak-to-strong axis is wanted. Defer Connections, Pictionary's vision path, and all social-deduction games.

# Adopt for Sei

## (a) Best technique stack for personality-varied play + skill calibration

Division of labor:
- LLM (any provider, including Anthropic default): speech, intent, persona flavor, narration, and direct play of language/knowledge/social/vision games.
- Per-game engine (small, per game): correctness and the skill dial for any exact-state or search game; the LLM narrates the engine's move in character.
- Activation steering / persona vectors: local-model path only, optional premium personality dial.

Personality direction (what they DO, not how they talk):
1. Replace adjective personas with explicit objective functions plus a 3 to 4 line weighted priority rubric the brain reasons over before dispatching tools (curiosity/loyalty/caution/greed weights). This is the evidence-backed way to move actions (economic-game literature; rubric-as-reward).
2. Bias tool selection per persona (curious -> find/goto/explore; cautious -> equip/sleep/retreat; greedy -> gather/container ops).
3. Name a target risk posture explicitly; the risk axis is the most steerable and cheaply yields visibly different play.
4. Re-inject the persona objective into the per-turn snapshot to fight drift (repeated interactions wash out persona).
5. Expect the fair/helpful/risk-neutral RLHF attractor; selfish/reckless/lazy personas need the strongest counter-framing. Verify separation on Haiku/Ollama before shipping.

Behavioral variance (how widely each persona explores):
6. Per-persona sampling profiles. On local/Ollama/OpenAI-compat: min-p (~0.05 to 0.1) + temperature (~1.0 to 1.5), higher for chaotic personas, lower for methodical. On Anthropic/Gemini (no min-p): Verbalized Sampling at the prompt layer to recover ~2x diversity.
7. Light repetition/frequency penalty to stop action loops.
8. Rely on the Zod registry as the validity guarantee so higher entropy is safe; decouple "which action" (high entropy) from "valid arguments" (schema-constrained). Avoid greedy/beam for the action-choosing call.
9. Optional lightweight adaptation: a bandit (UCB1 / Thompson) over a small per-persona strategy-arm set, with distinct priors per persona, for online adaptation without training.

Skill calibration:
10. Do NOT ask the LLM to self-handicap; it cannot do so reliably (sandbagging, difficulty-control, and "play like a beginner" evidence). Put skill in an engine/sampling parameter.
11. For engine games, prefer human-like error (errors scale with position difficulty, pick lower-evaluated moves) over uniform engine-noise or epsilon-random blunders. The Maia/Maia-2 approach (human-imitation, skill-conditioned) is the believability gold standard; emulate its spirit even where a full Maia model is unavailable.

## (b) Games to ship (with imagery source, scoring, and skill-variation method)

1. GeoGuessr clone (required).
   - Imagery: Mapillary API v4 (free, CC BY-SA, cacheable) primary; Google Street View Static (BYO key) optional HD mode. Validate locations with the free metadata/radius endpoints. Pre-curate a vetted pano-ID pool offline; sample via GeoNames populated places (country-weighted) + Natural Earth land rejection + imagery rejection.
   - Map: MapLibre GL JS + MapTiler key, or bundled PMTiles for offline.
   - Scoring: 5000 * exp(-d_km / 2000), haversine R=6371, under 25 m -> 5000; per-round timer.
   - Play/skill: vision LLM (Claude/Gemini) plays from the scene JPEG and outputs lat/lng + reasoning; handicap for fun (lower resolution, no EXIF, thinking-time cap, casual persona) since frontier models are very strong; surface reasoning as companion chatter via the say() tool. Reference ceiling: PIGEON (arXiv:2307.05845); LLM benchmark: GeoBench.

2. Codenames (co-op Duet).
   - No external imagery; word list only.
   - Skill: engine-side embedding spymaster (GloVe/word2vec/Sentence-Transformers, tuned by model quality + risk threshold) or LLM-side (allow/forbid assassin reasoning). Personality via clue style and risk appetite.
   - Co-op: Sei as your spymaster or guessing partner, shared timer. Matches the invite/involve-the-player direction.

3. 20 Questions.
   - No assets; turn counter + yes/no parser + registry tools.
   - Skill: LLM-side EIG/bisection scaffold (strong) vs stripped scaffold + leaked/corrupted prior answers (believably weak). Personality via question cadence and guess bravado.
   - Co-op and versus, symmetric. Ship answerer role first.

4. Optional fourth: Connect Four, when a crisp demonstrable weak-to-strong slider is wanted. Engine-side skill (solved oracle, search depth / epsilon-greedy); LLM narrates moves in character. Versus only.

Defer: Connections (LLMs genuinely cannot, no believable strong mode; content licensing), Pictionary vision-guesser (build + VLM ceiling; describer-mode is the cheap alternative), and all social-deduction games (multiplayer, architecturally at odds with single-companion single-loop).
