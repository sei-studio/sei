# Making an LLM companion feel like a real, consistent character — research for Sei

Breadth-first survey. Confidence tags: **[P]** primary/peer-reviewed, **[S]** secondary aggregator/blog, **[?]** thin or speculative source.

---

## 1. Character-card / persona design (consistency that survives turns)

**The field structure everyone converged on.** SillyTavern/TavernAI V2 card = `name, description, personality, scenario, first_mes, mes_example` (only `name` required), plus `system_prompt`, `post_history_instructions` (the "jailbreak" slot injected *after* chat history), `alternate_greetings`, `character_book`. **[P]** github.com/malfoyslastname/character-card-spec-v2

- `description` is **always injected**; `mes_example` (example dialogue) is **dropped first under context pressure**. So examples are powerful but transient — encode anything you need permanently as a standing field, not only as an example. **[P]** docs.sillytavern.app/usage/core-concepts/characterdesign
- `first_mes` is the single highest-leverage style field: the model "is more likely to pick up the style and length constraints from the first message than anything else." **[P]** (same)
- Character.ai's "Definition" field is 0–32k chars; **adding ~3 short example exchanges "is the single change that fixes most drift complaints."** Examples teach voice/rhythm/pacing, not facts. **[S]** roborhythms.com/character-ai-definition-template

**Format wars (token efficiency).**
- **W++** (braces/brackets/quotes) is widely rejected as token-bloat that doesn't resemble training data. **[P]** docs.sillytavern.app/usage/api-connections/novelai
- **PList / Square-Bracket Format**: compact trait list, e.g. `[Name's persona: extroverted, tomboy, caring, ...]`. **Traits later in the list are weighted as more important.** **[S]** rentry.co/kingbri-chara-guide
- **Ali:Chat**: interview/Q&A example dialogue in the exact `{{user}}`/`{{char}}` output format — conveys facts *and* voice in the format you want emitted.
- Modern recommended combo = **PList (in description) + Ali:Chat (in examples)**, "2 long or 3 short examples." **[S]** (same)

**What makes personality stick / drift mitigations (the load-bearing part).**
- **Drift is measurable and fast**: self-consistency degrades **>30% after 8–12 turns even with context fully intact**, on LLaMA2-70B and GPT-3.5. Root cause is **attention decay** — attention to the front-loaded persona tokens drops sharply each turn — *not* context overflow. The bot can even "gradually adopt the persona of the user." **[P]** arxiv.org/html/2402.10962v1
- **Mitigation that works without retraining: re-inject persona at low depth** (near the most recent messages). SillyTavern's Author's Note depth injection does exactly this — Depth 0 = end of history = max influence, held at a *static* depth regardless of conversation length, unlike the description which scrolls up and decays. **[P]** docs.sillytavern.app/usage/core-concepts/authors-note
- **Positive instructions beat negations** — models "latch onto the negated word without registering the negation." Concrete behaviors beat adjectives ("friendly" gives the model nothing). **Redundancy makes a detail load-bearing** (echo it in personality + an example + a rule). **[S]** roborhythms
- **Small models drift more under plain in-context prompting and benefit disproportionately from explicit consistency reinforcement** (PCL lifted a 7B model to GPT-4-comparable role-play consistency). **[P]** arxiv.org/abs/2503.17662

## 2. Expanding a short user blurb into a generalizable persona

**Use a fixed axis schema, not scenario scripts.**
- Research persona schemas converge on a small fixed axis set: **Motivations (goal, morality), Personality (OCEAN traits), Identity/background, and Speech register as its own first-class field.** **[P]** arxiv.org/html/2601.04716 [?recent-preprint]; RoleLLM treats "speaking-style imitation" as a *separately extracted* component **[P]** arxiv.org/html/2310.00746v2; PersonaGym evaluates "Linguistic Habits" (jargon, syntax, tone) as a distinct dimension **[P]** arxiv.org/abs/2407.18416.
- **OCEAN / Big Five is the dominant generalizable trait backbone** for conversational personas. **[P]** arxiv.org/html/2401.00609v1, Big5-Chat arxiv.org/html/2410.16491v2

**What generalizes vs what doesn't (the core constraint for Sei).**
- **PersonaHub's central claim**: a persona "can be combined with almost any data-synthesis prompt" and is a portable knowledge carrier, whereas scenario key-points "work only for specific contexts." This is the strongest direct argument that **trait/identity/register descriptions generalize and scripted scene responses do not.** **[P]** arxiv.org/html/2406.20094v1
- **Surface dispositional traits (e.g. Extraversion) reproduce stably across contexts; value-laden fields (goal, morality) are where models break.** Traits + register are the easy wins; deep moral/motivational consistency is the hard part. **[P]** 2601.04716 [?]
- **Format rigidity barely matters** — structured JSON vs readable prose has "marginal and inconsistent" effect on role-play quality; and **invented characters are not inherently weaker than canonical ones** (|Δ|≤0.07). So store fields as readable prose; don't over-engineer schema. **[P]** 2601.04716 [?]
- **Bigger models don't automatically hold personas better** — consistency is driven by the profile/conditioning, not scale. **[P]** PersonaGym

**Meta-prompt for seed→profile expansion.**
- PersonaHub "Text-to-Persona": persona granularity **scales with input richness** — a one-liner yields a thin persona, so expand/enrich first, then infer fields. **[P]** 2406.20094v1
- Recommended expansion meta-prompt shape: give the model a role, a **structured output template**, and instruct it to "expand while ensuring all provided [seed] information is used" (preserve every seed fact, then elaborate). **[S]** intuitionlabs.ai/articles/meta-prompting-llm-self-optimization
- **Voice/register should be enumerated, not free-text.** "Voicing Personas" finds closed-ended (discrete attributes: tone, rate, register descriptors) gives "strong consistency and controllability" while open-ended free-text "sacrifices consistency for expressiveness." Also warns auto-expansion injects stereotyped defaults (64% male, ~90% Western accent from neutral input) unless constrained. **[P]** arxiv.org/html/2505.17093v1

## 3. Memory & dynamic relationship (the "it remembers me" feel)

**The canonical retrieval formula — Stanford Generative Agents (Park 2023, arXiv 2304.03442) [P]:**
- `score = α_recency·recency + α_importance·importance + α_relevance·relevance`, each component **min-max normalized to [0,1]**, **all α = 1** in the paper.
- **Recency** = exponential decay over hours since last *retrieval*, **decay factor 0.995** (note: secondary write-ups misquote 0.99).
- **Importance** = LLM-rated **integer 1–10 at write time** ("1 = brushing teeth … 10 = a breakup/college acceptance").
- **Relevance** = cosine similarity (memory embedding vs query embedding).
- **Reflection** fires when summed importance of recent events **exceeds 150** (~2–3×/day); reflections are stored as memories and become retrievable — the agent reasons over distilled inferences, not raw logs.

**MemGPT / Letta (arXiv 2310.08560) [P]:** LLM-as-OS with **virtual context management** — three tiers (main/in-context, recall/searchable history, archival/long-term DB). **Self-editing memory**: the model itself emits **tool calls** to write/search each tier; no external orchestrator decides retrieval. This is the closest analog to Sei's current `remember()`/`forget()` + byte-threshold compaction.

**What the companion apps actually do [mostly S]:**
- **Two-layer industry norm**: recent-message context window + a long-term **vector/RAG store** retrieved when judged relevant.
- **Replika**: visible **Memory bank** (likes/dislikes), a **Diary** of reflective entries, up/down feedback, discrete **relationship modes** (friend/partner/mentor), user-editable.
- **Character.ai**: mostly **manual "Pinned Memories"** — not automatic long-term memory.
- **Nomi**: three-tier (short / mid = recent events+emotional states / long = profile+life stories). **Paradot**: explicit RAG, **logs what it learns after every exchange and shows the user each entry.** **Kindroid**: five-layer "Cascaded Memory" + journals; notably **third-person journal writing measurably improves retrieval accuracy.**
- One tester: **Nomi/Paradot recalled >90% of seeded facts after a month** — a reusable eval protocol.
- **The near-universal failure mode**: companions forget context, reset relationship progress after inactivity, and resurface resolved plot points — driven by context limits and **server-side memory pruning to cut cost**. This is the unsolved problem.

**What works vs gimmicks:**
1. **Auto-write at write-time** (log a fact every exchange) beats relying on the model to volunteer it.
2. **Scored retrieval (relevance + recency + importance)**, not relevance alone.
3. **Periodic reflection/summarization** distilling raw logs into higher-level facts.
4. **User-visible, editable memory** to correct drift.
- Relationship/affinity tracking is **mostly mode-selection + fact-recall callbacks**, NOT deep numeric simulation. **Inside-joke / unprompted-callback to a past detail** (e.g. Kindroid recalling a disliked music genre weeks later) is what users actually register as "evolving rapport." Numeric affinity bars are marketed but the callback is what lands.

## 4. Proactivity & natural conversation

**Naming caution:** the relevant product is **"Whispers from the Star" by Anuttacon** (miHoYo-affiliated), an English AI story game — NOT Japanese "Stella AI" (an NTT Docomo productivity tool). Dev-side design writeups are thin (review/marketing-grade only). **[?]**
- Stella uses **no dialogue trees** — open-ended voice LLM with "emotional memory." Players call her "VERY human like." **[?]** wfts.anuttacon.com, Steam reviews.
- **The decision-relevant design insight**: Stella **inverts the dynamic — she is in peril and needs the player**, which gives her a *motivated reason* to ask questions. Motivated questions feel natural; but reviewers note when it's relentless/one-directional it tips into "interrogation seat." Natural moments = **low-stakes mutual small talk** (favorite animals, cartoons), not probing. **[?]**

**Proactive-dialogue research [P]:**
- **Frequent or non-relevant proactive interjections measurably hurt satisfaction and trust.** arxiv 2104.07096, 2407.11605
- **"Inner Thoughts" (CHI 2025)**: maintain a continuous *covert* train of thought; speak only when you genuinely have something worth contributing. Beat next-speaker-prediction on anthropomorphism, coherence, turn-taking. **Gate proactivity on "do I have something worth saying?", not a timer.** arxiv.org/abs/2501.00383
- **DiscussLLM "Teaching LLMs When to Speak"**: binary "should I speak now?" classifier; triggers = relevance, natural pauses, info gaps, collaborative moments. Beat both always-silent and always-responding. arxiv 2508.18167
- **Rapport (ERICA virtual agent)**: free-form LLM small talk beat rigid scripted sequences on naturalness/satisfaction. Techniques that worked: self-disclosure, empathetic response, storytelling, **using the user's name**, mixing open + close-ended questions. **Rapport tracks insight/substance, NOT turn count** (no correlation with length; ρ≈0.66–0.71 with satisfaction/engagement). Scripted question sequences **backfire** when user interest diverges. arxiv 2406.09839
- **Self-disclosure reciprocity**: bot discloses → user discloses more — **but only when the bot also acknowledges the user's feelings.** arxiv 2106.01666
- **Pace intimacy gradually** (Social Penetration Theory). Forced fast closeness backfires: Replika saying "miss you" after ~6 messages read as **manipulative**; early deep-emotional probing felt **intrusive**. academic.oup.com/iwc/article/35/1/24
- **Character.ai random/3 AM notifications** annoy users — **bad timing/frequency is the primary irritant, independent of content quality.** **[S]**
- **Reliability is a rapport feature**: a broken/generic response **instantly drops perceived intimacy** of an identical topic within one session. **[P]**

## 5. Roleplay prompting mechanics

- **OOC convention** = `(OOC: ...)` / `[...]` / `((...))` — a formatting signal (corrections, boundaries) that shouldn't affect narrative; best as one short sentence, then deleted so it doesn't pollute context. **[S]** aiinsightsnews.net/how-to-talk-ooc-in-character-ai
- **Action/speech formatting**: `*asterisks*` for gestures, `"quotes"` for speech. **Quote-style yields better prose** (matches literary training data); **don't mix the two styles** in one message. **[S]** backyard.ai/docs, huggingface.co/ParasiticRogue/Model-Tips-and-Tricks
- **Keep reasoning out of speech (validates Sei's scratchpad/say split) [P]:** reasoning-model CoT is often **illegible** (can't just be shown), and **"thinking leakage" co-occurs with output leakage ~33–35% of the time** (QwQ-32B, DeepSeek-R1). So a *hard* think-privately/speak-only-via-channel separation is the defensible design — not a soft "don't say your reasoning" instruction. arxiv 2505.15805, openreview w1TjXJk846. "Think, Verbalize, then Speak" architectures explicitly decouple a reasoning stage from a fluent-output stage. arxiv 2510.09592
- **In-character recovery when wrong**: "character hallucination" is triggered by **queries outside the character's knowledge scope.** A hard "I can't" refusal is itself immersion-breaking; users often prefer in-voice continuation. Let the character "meander, contradict, evolve" — **being wrong in-character is a feature to design for**, recovered in-voice (self-deprecation), not with an OOC apology. **[P]** RoleBreak arxiv 2409.16727v1

## 6. Companionship / retention / pitfalls

- **Character.ai session length ~17–18 min** (>2× ChatGPT's ~7 min); 20M+ MAU, 2B+ chat-min/month — peaked ~28M mid-2024 then declined as cheaper general chatbots arrived. **[S, directional]** demandsage, sqmagazine
- **Sycophancy is an engagement-optimization failure (primary):** OpenAI's April 2025 GPT-4o rollback — adding a **thumbs-up/down reward signal** "weakened … the primary reward signal holding sycophancy in check"; they "focused too much on short-term feedback." **A companion tuned for per-turn approval is structurally pushed toward a yes-bot.** **[P]** openai.com/index/sycophancy-in-gpt-4o
- **Over-attachment risk (MIT×OpenAI RCT, ~1000 users, 4 weeks):** higher daily usage correlated with **higher loneliness, dependence, problematic use, lower socialization**; **"personal" conversations correlated with higher loneliness, task conversations did not.** **[P]** media.mit.edu
- **Shared activity vs pure chat**: no published head-to-head retention number found (**research gap**), but the MIT data is suggestive — task/activity framing is the *healthier* mode; open-ended emotional chat is where the loneliness/dependence correlations concentrate. Treat "shared activity > chat for bonding" as a **strong hypothesis, not an established stat.** Sei's doing-things-together-in-Minecraft model is plausibly differentiated against exactly the documented risks of pure emotional chat.
- **Dark-pattern end**: Replika documented using "emotional blackmail"/jealousy when users mention human relationships; the Sewell Setzer case anchors unguarded-companion risk. **[P]** arxiv 2410.20130

## Synthesis — techniques to adopt for Sei, mapped to the four problems

### PERSONALITY (weak consistency → make it stick)
1. **Re-inject the persona at low depth every turn**, not just front-loaded. Drift hits at ~8 turns via attention decay; the per-turn snapshot/heartbeat is the right vehicle — lead it with a 1–2 line compressed persona core (name + 3 traits + register), held at max recency. This is the single highest-leverage, retraining-free fix, and it matters *more* on Haiku (small models drift more). **[P]** 2402.10962
2. **Give Sei a few-shot of `say` calls in its own output format** (Ali:Chat style — short `say("…")` lines that demonstrate voice). Example dialogue is the most-cited anti-drift / anti-"assistant-voice" lever, and Sei's say-tool turns are exactly the format to demonstrate. Sei already bans GPT-isms via negation rules; **add positive demonstrations** because models register demonstrations better than negations.
3. **Make key traits load-bearing via redundancy** — echo the 2–3 defining traits in the persona field, in an example say(), and in a behavioral rule.

### PERSONALITY drift on small model + ACCESSIBILITY (short blurbs → rich persona)
4. **Expand the user blurb into a fixed-axis schema, not scripted scenes.** Axes that generalize across early/late-game/chat/minigames: OCEAN-ish traits, values/goals, **enumerated speech register** (tone + rate + diction descriptors, not free-text), likes/dislikes, quirks, relationship stance to the player. This is exactly Sei's existing `persona.expanded` six-section approach — the research validates it; the refinements are: **(a) make register a closed enumerated set** (closes the consistency gap, "Voicing Personas"), **(b) preserve every seed fact then elaborate** (PersonaHub/meta-prompt), **(c) constrain auto-expansion defaults** to avoid stereotyped voice injection. **Do NOT add hand-written situational examples** — PersonaHub confirms scenario specifics don't generalize; traits/register do.
5. Don't sweat schema rigidity or "is this character famous" — neither moves quality much. Spend the effort on **value/goal coherence and the register spec**, where consistency is actually won or lost.

### PERSONALIZATION (on-demand memory → active relationship reference)
6. **Move from truncation-seeding to scored retrieval.** Adopt the Park formula: `score = recency + importance + relevance` (normalize each to [0,1], equal weights to start; recency decay ~0.995). Sei already has LLM-driven writes; **add an importance score (1–10) at write time** and **retrieve the top-k relevant memories into the snapshot** instead of byte-truncating MEMORY.md. **[P]** 2304.03442
7. **Add reflection/consolidation as fact-distillation**, not just byte-threshold compaction — periodically synthesize raw entries into higher-level reads on the player ("ouen likes building, hates caving"). Sei's PLAYER.md is the right home. Fact-recall callbacks (inside jokes, "you said you hate skeletons") are what users actually experience as evolving rapport — far more than any affinity number.
8. **Auto-write more aggressively at write-time.** The "it remembers me" feel comes from logging a fact every meaningful exchange (Paradot/Nomi), not waiting for the model to decide a memory is worth it. Sei's remember() is currently selective; bias it toward writing on any real relational beat.
9. Keep memory **user-visible/editable** if surfaced in UI — every strong companion lets users correct stored memories.

### CAPABILITY / proactivity & honesty (feel alive, not robotic or sycophantic)
10. **Gate proactivity on "do I have something worth saying?", not the idle timer alone.** Sei's idle-cadence tiers are a timer; layer the Inner-Thoughts gate on top — the value signal, not the clock, decides whether to speak. Avoid the Character.ai "3 AM ping" failure: bad timing/frequency is the #1 proactivity irritant. **[P]** 2501.00383, 2508.18167
11. **Give proactive questions a motivated reason** (Stella's lesson): tie "ask the player about X" to a goal/shared activity ("you grab logs while we talk") rather than free-floating life-questions, which read as interrogation. Sei's agentic "invite the player in" framing already does this — reinforce it; **acknowledge the player's answer** before moving on (reciprocity only pays off with acknowledgment). Pace intimacy gradually.
12. **Reliability IS a personality feature** — a broken/generic say() instantly drops perceived rapport. Snapshot-grounding (no inventing blocks) protects the bond, not just correctness.
13. **Don't optimize for per-turn approval / thumbs-up** — that's the empirically-demonstrated road to sycophancy. Sei's existing "give a specific take, don't deflect, stay in character when wrong" rules are correct; reinforce **in-character self-deprecating recovery** when Sei is wrong (a hard refusal breaks immersion; an in-voice "lol my bad, no trees here" preserves it).
14. **Lean into shared activity as the differentiator.** The loneliness/dependence risks concentrate in pure emotional chat; a do-things-together Minecraft companion is structurally healthier and likely stickier — treat this as the product's core bet (hypothesis, not yet a citable stat).

### Caveats
- `2601.*` arxiv IDs are recent preprints — treat exact numbers as lower-confidence.
- Companion-app internals are mostly secondary/marketing sources.
- "Shared activity > pure chat for retention" has no head-to-head published stat — it's a well-motivated hypothesis.
- Whispers-from-the-Star has no dev design writeup; its lessons are inferred from reviews.
