## Gaps Summary

**One partial gap surfaced — Bucket A item 16 (Plan 10 WR-04):**

The `preservedInterrupt` shape captured at sei:attacked time forwards `chatText` correctly but loses the chatter's `username`. The chat-arrival branch at `src/brain/orchestrator.js:615-618` captures `who` into a local variable but does NOT include it in the `pendingInterrupt = { chatText: String(chatText) }` object. When sei:attacked later reads `pendingInterrupt.who`, it's always `undefined`; the fallback `data?.who` (attack event payload) is also always undefined; the re-enqueued `sei:chat_received` therefore gets `username: 'owner'` literal. This mis-attributes the chat in `recent_owner_chat`, breaks pushOwner's per-speaker bucketing, and defeats the dedup signature.

**Severity:** PARTIAL on Bucket A truth #16. Plan 10's intent (preserve chat through attack) is partially achieved — the text survives, the speaker identity does not.

**Fix scope:** ~3 lines in `src/brain/orchestrator.js` + a regression test. Recommended for a follow-up `/gsd-plan-phase 03.1 --gaps` cycle alongside the 4 review-WARNINGs (WR-02 loopHasAffect over-match, WR-03 ts dedup, WR-04 bridgeExternalAbort early-return, WR-05 test gap).
