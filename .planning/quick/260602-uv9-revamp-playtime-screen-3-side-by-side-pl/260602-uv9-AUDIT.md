**Scope:** Confirm the Polar billing path is consistent with the new "no refunds" customer-facing

**Files reviewed:**

## Finding 1 — Cancellation is end-of-period, non-prorated: **CONFIRMED**

`apply_polar_event` maps cancellation to "stop auto-renewal at the end of the current paid
period," never a mid-period proration or refund:

- **cancel-at-period-end on an active sub** — `subscription.created / .active / .updated /
  .uncanceled` arm: line **230** maps
  `v_polar_status in ('active','trialing') AND v_cancel_eop` → `'cancelled'`, and lines
  **240–241** set `ends_at = coalesce(v_ends_at, v_renews_at)` (i.e. usable until the period
  ends).
  (`v_cancel_eop` is derived from `cancel_at_period_end` at line **180**.)
- **explicit `subscription.canceled`** — arm at line **249**; comment line **250**
  ("Cancellation scheduled — still usable until the period ends"); sets `status = 'cancelled'`
  with `ends_at = coalesce(v_ends_at, v_renews_at)` (lines **255**, **260**).
- **`subscription.revoked`** (definitive end) → `'expired'` (lines **262–273**).

There is **no proration arithmetic and no automatic refund** in any cancellation arm. This already
matches the Task 6 terms.html §8 wording ("Cancellation stops auto-renewal at the end of the
current paid billing period; you keep access through the end of that period. We do not pro-rate or
refund partial-month cancellations.").

## Finding 2 — A refund-clawback path **EXISTS** (surfaced deliberately)

This contradicts the audit's naive "no DB change, no refund logic anywhere" expectation, so it is
called out explicitly:

- **polar-webhook allowlist** includes `'order.refunded'` — `supabase/functions/polar-webhook/index.ts`
  line **76** (ALLOWED_EVENTS); documented at lines **30–34**.
- **`order.refunded` handler** — `apply_polar_event`, migration lines **286–310**:
  - looks up the original grant's `credits_micro` by `provider_event_id = order_id` (lines **290–294**);
  - inserts a compensating **NEGATIVE** ledger grant `kind = 'refund'`,
    `credits_micro = -v_orig_micro`, `source = 'polar_refund'` (lines **300–304**),
    idempotent via `on conflict (provider_event_id) do nothing`;
  - if the refunded order was a subscription, sets `subscription_status.status = 'refunded'`
    and `ends_at = coalesce(v_ends_at, now())` (lines **305–310**).

So an upstream refund DOES claw back the ledger balance and flips the subscription to `'refunded'`.

---

## Disposition — **KEEP the clawback handler. NO DB migration.**

**Decision: KEEP `order.refunded`; no DB change required.**

**Rationale:**
- Polar is the **Merchant of Record (MoR)**. Even though Sei does not OFFER refunds, Polar can
  still issue one out-of-band from its own dashboard — for a chargeback, fraud reversal, or a
  legal / consumer-protection obligation that Polar (not Sei) is bound by as MoR.
- If such an MoR-issued refund fired and Sei had **no** `order.refunded` handler, the user would
  keep ledger credits they were refunded for — leaving the ledger **inconsistent** with the money
  actually held. That is strictly worse than keeping the handler.
- "No refunds" is a **customer-facing posture** (Sei does not OFFER refunds), **not** a guarantee
  that the ledger ignores a refund the Merchant of Record decides to issue. The clawback handler is
  a safety / consistency mechanism that reconciles the ledger when a refund happens upstream.

This matches threat-register **T-uv9-06** (`accept`): KEEP the `order.refunded` clawback so an
MoR-issued refund reconciles the ledger.

**No migration authored.** No speculative SQL written. No MCP/Supabase mutation executed (read-only
audit).

---

## Operator option (out of scope unless explicitly requested)

If the operator instead wants to **hard-block all refunds** — so that a refund issued from the
Polar dashboard leaves the Sei ledger untouched (user keeps the credits, ledger unchanged) — that
is a deliberate future change to the `order.refunded` arm of `apply_polar_event` (e.g. early-return
/ no-op that arm, or drop `'order.refunded'` from the webhook ALLOWED_EVENTS). That change is
**out of scope for this quick task** and should only be made on an explicit operator request, with
its own migration, because it intentionally lets the ledger diverge from MoR-held funds.
